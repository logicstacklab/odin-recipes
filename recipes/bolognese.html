Authentic Italian Bolognese Meat Sauce

Certified Recipe for Traditional Ragù alla Bolognese

AThe Accademia Italiana della Cucina is a cultural institution recognized by the Italian Republic. Founded in 1953 by Orio Vergani, its mission is to preserve and promote Italy’s culinary traditions.

On April 20, 2023, the Academy officially submitted the updated version of the authentic Ragù alla Bolognese recipe to the Bologna Chamber of Commerce. This action reinforces its commitment to protecting traditional Italian cuisine and ensuring its authenticity.

Ingredients (Serves 6)

  • Coarsely ground beef (see note): 1 lb (400g)
  • Fresh pork pancetta, slices: 6 oz (150g)
  • ½ onion, peeled: about 2 oz (60g)
  • 1 medium carrot, peeled: about 2 oz (60g)
  • 1 celery stalk, trimmed: about 2 oz (60g)
  • ½ cup (1 glass) of red or white wine
  • Strained tomatoes: 7 oz (200g)
  • Tomato paste (double-concentrated): 1 tbsp
  • ½ cup (1 glass) of whole milk (optional)
  • Light meat or vegetable broth (or stock cubes)
  • Extra virgin olive oil: 3 tbsp
  • Salt and pepper

Steps

  1. In a heavy-bottomed casserole or Dutch oven (24–26 cm / 10 inches), gently melt the chopped pancetta with the olive oil over low heat.
  2. Finely chop the onion, carrot, and celery using a knife only. Add them to the pan and cook slowly, stirring with a wooden spoon, until softened but not browned.
  3. Increase the heat to medium, add the ground beef, and break it up. Cook for about ten minutes, stirring frequently, until the meat is lightly browned.
  4. Pour in the wine and let it cook until fully evaporated.
  5. Stir in the tomato paste and strained tomatoes. Add one cup of hot broth or water, cover, and let the sauce simmer gently for about two hours (up to three, depending on preference), adding more liquid as needed.
  6. If using milk, add it halfway through the cooking time and allow it to fully evaporate.
  7. Season with salt and pepper at the end. The finished sauce should be thick, glossy, and a deep reddish-brown color.
