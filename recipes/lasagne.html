Yellow bolognese lasagne (or golden drop lasagne)

Lasagne from the Emilia-Romagna region

Lasagne are both a type of pasta in the form of rectangular sheets and an Italian dish made of alternating layers of lasagne pasta, Parmesan, mozzarella or ricotta, Bolognese sauce, and béchamel sauce, baked in the oven.

Originating from central-southern Italy, they have been adapted worldwide into many variations, including vegetable, spinach, eggplant, pesto, meat, fish, seafood, and vegetarian versions.

Ingredients (Serves 4)

  • For the pastry:

    • 400 g of 00 flour
    • 4 eggs

  • For the filling:

    • 300 g of classic Bolognese ragù
    • 100 g of sweetbreads
    • 150 g of chicken livers a few bay leaves

  • For the dressing:

    • 300 g of grated parmesan cheese
    • 250 g of cream
    • 100 g of butter nutmeg
    • salt and pepper

  • For the sautéed mushrooms:

    • 400 g of porcini mushrooms
    • 2 cloves of garlic
    • 1 handful of chopped parsley olive oil
    • salt and pepper

Steps

  1. Cook a standard Bolognese ragù with bay leaves. Add diced sweetbreads, then after 15 minutes add the livers and cook another 15 minutes.
  2. Wash, dry, and slice the mushrooms. Brown them in hot oil with minced garlic, then season with salt, pepper, and parsley
  3. Roll thin pasta sheets and cut into 10–15 cm rectangles. Cook in boiling salted water with a little olive oil until al dente, then drain and pat dry.
  4. Butter a baking dish. Layer pasta, cream, ragù, mushrooms, and Parmesan in alternating layers as follows:
    1st layer: pasta, cream, ragù, Parmesan
    2nd layer: pasta, cream, mushrooms, Parmesan
    3rd layer: pasta, cream, ragù, Parmesan
    4th layer: pasta, cream, mushrooms, Parmesan
    Top with a final layer of pasta, cream, and Parmesan.
  5. Cover with parchment paper and bake at 180°C (350°F) for 30 minutes, then broil 10 minutes. Let rest 10 minutes, cut, and serve.
